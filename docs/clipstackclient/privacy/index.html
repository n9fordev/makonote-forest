ClipStackClient Privacy Policy

ClipStackClient is designed to work with ClipStack on macOS.
This page describes how the current release handles data on iPhone and iPad.

Current policy

Overview

ClipStackClient respects your privacy.
The app reads encrypted synced history generated by ClipStack so you can browse and copy synced text on your mobile device.

Information handled by the app

  • Synced text clips from ClipStack
  • Pinned state included in the synced history
  • A locally available shared sync key from iCloud Keychain
  • Locally cached encrypted sync data for offline fallback

How sync works in the current release

The current release reads synced history from your CloudKit private database.
ClipStackClient does not upload your clipboard history to a server operated by the developer.

Local storage

The app may keep an encrypted local cache of the latest synced payload so previously available content can still be shown when an update fails.

The shared sync key may be made available through iCloud Keychain so the synced history can be decrypted locally.

Clipboard access

When you tap a clip, ClipStackClient copies that selected item back to the system clipboard on your device.

Data collection

ClipStackClient does not require an account.
The current release does not include analytics SDKs, advertising SDKs, or a developer-operated backend service.

Your choices

  • Turn off ClipStackClient sync in ClipStack on your Mac
  • Turn off iCloud Keychain or stop using the sync workflow
  • Delete or change synced content from ClipStack on your Mac
