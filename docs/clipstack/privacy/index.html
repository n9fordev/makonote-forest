ClipStacks Privacy Policy

ClipStacks is designed as a local-first clipboard utility.
This page describes how the current release handles data on macOS.

Current policy

Overview

ClipStacks respects your privacy.
Clipboard history is stored locally on your device to provide the app’s core functionality.

Information we handle

  • Text
  • Links
  • Images

This content is used only to provide clipboard history features inside the app.

Local storage

Clipboard history is stored locally on your device.
You can clear history at any time, and you can disable persistent history in Settings.

Data collection

ClipStacks does not require a user account.
The current release does not upload clipboard history to a developer-operated remote server.

If you enable ClipStackClient sync in Settings, ClipStacks uploads text-only encrypted sync data to your CloudKit private database so your own Apple devices can access it.

Sharing

ClipStacks does not share clipboard history with third parties
as part of its core local-only behavior.

Your choices

  • Disable persistent history
  • Delete individual history items
  • Clear all history
